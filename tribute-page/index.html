J. R. R. Tolkien

An inspiration to painters, musicians, film-makers and writers

The life of J. R. R. Tolkien:

  • 1892 - Born in Bloomfontein, South Africa
  • 1904 - at age 12, his mother Mabel died from diabetes affecting both his brother and himself emotionally
  • 1908 - the boys move to Duchess Road, live with Mrs. Faulkner and Tolkien meets Edith Bratt, another lodger
  • 1909 - Father Francis discovers the romance between Tolkien and Edith, Ronald fails scholarship to Oxford
  • 1909 - around the 10th of June, he composed "The Book of the Foxrook"
  • 1910 - Tolkien brothers move to new lodgings, Ronald's romance with Edith continues until Father Francis forbids him from communicating with her, Edith moves to Cheltenham
  • 1911 - while in King Edward's School, formed a secret society named T.C.B.S. (Tea Club and Barrovian Society) with three friends, Rob Gilson, Geoffrey Bache Smith and Christopher Wiseman, spent the summer in Switzerland where Bilbo's journey to the Misty Mountains was based on and entered Exeter College, Oxford, on October of the same year
  • 1913 - changed course to English Language and Literature, writes to Edith on his 21st birthday and soon after, become engaged
  • 1914 - Tolkien and Edith become betrothed after she is received into the Catholic Church, Tolkien writes his first indentifiable "Middle-earth" fragment 'The Voyage of Éarendel the Evening Star’
  • 1915 - graduated with first-class honours in his final examinations, final meeting of the four main members of T.C.B.S. in Lichfield
  • 1916 - Ronald and Edith are married in Warwick, Battle of the Somme begins. Rob Gilson, friend and member of T.C.B.S. dies. Tolkien reports sick and returns to England
  • 1917 - Tolkien is inspired by watching Edith dance in a 'hemlock' glade near Roos. Germ for meeting of Lúthien and Beren in his legendarium.
  • 1918 - The Tolkiens return to Oxford. Tolkien obtains employment with the New English Dictionary [Oxford English Dictionary]. Tolkien probably begins work on the first version of ‘The Music of the Ainur’.
  • 1920 - He begins work as a Reader in English Language at Leeds University. Michael, the Tolkiens’ second son is born on 22 October.
  • 1922 - A Middle English Vocabulary is published. Tolkien and E.V. Gordon begin work on their edition of Sir Gawain and the Green Knight.
  • 1925 - Tolkien and E.V. Gordon’s edition of Sir Gawain and the Green Knight is published by the Clarendon Press. On holiday in Filey, Yorkshire, Tolkien relates the story of a toy dog, ‘Rover’, which is posthumously published as Roverandom. Tolkien takes up an appointment as Rawlinson and Bosworth Professor of Anglo-Saxon at Oxford. He purchases a house in Oxford.
  • 1930 - Tolkien may have written the first sentence of The Hobbit: "In a hole in the ground there lived a hobbit"
  • 1933 - The Inklings meetings under the auspices of C.S. Lewis become an important part of Tolkien's social life
  • 1937 - Beowulf: The Monsters and the Critics and The Hobbit are published. Because of the latter's success, Stanley Unwin urges Tolkien to write a sequel, this sparks the beginning of The Lord of the Rings
  • 1945 - Tolkien becomes Oxford's Merton Professor of English Language and Literature
  • 1947 - On Fairy-Stories is published in Essays Presented to Charles Williams
  • 1949 - Farmer Giles of Ham is published
  • 1953 - Publication of The Homecoming of Beorhtnoth Beorhthelm's Son
  • 1954 - Publication of The Fellowship of the Ring in July, then The Two Towers in November
  • 1955 - Publication of The Return of the King in October
  • 1963 - Tolkien's lecture English and Welsh is published, Death of C.S. Lewis
  • 1964 - Tree and Leaf is published
  • 1966 - Publication of The Tolkien Reader
  • 1967 - The Road Goes Ever On is published in the US in October and Smith of Wootton Major in November
  • 1971 - Death of Edith Tolkien
  • 1972 - Tolkien is awarded a CBE in the New Year's honours list
  • 1973 - Death of J.R.R. Tolkien from a stomach ulcer
